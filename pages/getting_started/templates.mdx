## What this feature does

Templates let you control how your meeting summaries look and what information they include. Instead of getting the same default format every time, you can choose or create templates that match how you work. This means you get summaries that are ready to use right away, with the sections and details you actually need.

<Info>
You don't need to set up anything special to use this feature. Templates are available from any meeting summary.
</Info>

## How to use it

### Applying a template to a meeting

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a meeting">
    Go to your meetings list and click on any meeting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the template dropdown">
    Look below the summary. You will see a dropdown showing the current template name (such as "Default").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a template">
    Click the dropdown to see your available templates. Click on any template to apply it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for the summary">
    Jamie will regenerate the summary using that template's format. A checkmark shows which template is currently applied.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Viewing all templates

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the template dropdown">
    From any meeting summary, click on the template dropdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click View All Templates">
    At the bottom of the dropdown, click **View All Templates**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse templates">
    Use the **Default** and **Personal** tabs to switch between pre-made templates and ones you have created. Click any template to see its details on the right side.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Creating a new template

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start creating">
    From the template dropdown, click **New template**. Or, from the templates window, click **New Template** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your starting point">
    You can either click **start from scratch** to create a blank template, or select one of the default templates as a starting point (Jamie will make a copy for you to edit).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your template">
    Give your template a name by editing the title at the top.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add meeting context">
    Fill in the **Meeting Context** field to describe when this template should be used (for example, "Weekly team sync meetings").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up sections">
    Add and edit **Sections**. Each section has a **title** (like "Key Decisions" or "Action Items") and **Instructions** that tell Jamie what to include in that section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize sections">
    - To add more sections, click **Add Section** at the bottom of the sections list.
    - To remove a section, hover over it and click the X button.
    - To reorder sections, drag them using the grip handle on the left side.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Your changes save automatically. No need to click a save button.
</Tip>

### Duplicating a default template

If you like one of the pre-made templates but want to customize it:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Default templates">
    Open the templates window and go to the **Default** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select and duplicate">
    Select the template you want to copy, then click the **Duplicate** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit your copy">
    A copy appears in your **Personal** templates that you can edit freely.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Deleting a template

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Personal templates">
    Open the templates window and go to the **Personal** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the template">
    Click on the template you want to delete.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete it">
    Click the menu button (three dots) in the top right of the editor, then click **Delete template**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips and common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I use multiple templates on the same meeting?">
    You can switch templates as many times as you like. Each time you apply a different template, Jamie regenerates the summary in that new format.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will applying a template change my transcript?">
    No. Templates only change how the summary is formatted. Your original transcript stays the same.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I edit the default templates?">
    Default templates are read-only. To customize one, use the **Duplicate** button to make your own copy, then edit that.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Who can see my personal templates?">
    Personal templates are private to your account. Only you can see and use them.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if I delete a template I've used before?">
    Meetings that used that template keep their existing summaries. You just won't be able to apply that template to new meetings.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I write good section instructions?">
    Be specific about what you want. For example, instead of "Action items," try "List all tasks mentioned with who is responsible and any deadlines discussed."
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Limits and things to keep in mind

<Warning>
Each template needs at least one section with a title and instructions to work properly.
</Warning>

- When you apply a template, it may take a few moments for Jamie to generate the new summary.
- Very long or complex templates may take longer to process.
- Templates work best when the meeting transcript has enough content for each section. If the meeting didn't cover a topic, that section may be brief or empty.
- You can only delete templates you created yourself.
